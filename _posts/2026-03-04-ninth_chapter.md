### Phile 9. Competing or Complementary? How the Cyber Kill Chain, MITRE ATT&CK, and the Diamond Model Work Together

After discussing the three most important CTI frameworks, let’s examine how they interconnect. Are they competitors, or do they complement each other?

In the real world, cyber threats evolve rapidly. Organizations collect millions of signals every day from their devices and networks. Reducing noise is critical. Security analysts are overwhelmed with alerts and continuously filter out false positives one after another.

In this environment, creating meaningful cyber threat intelligence is essential. These three frameworks play a crucial role in that effort. Organizations that learn how to properly integrate these tools into their workflow gain a significant advantage.

None of these frameworks is perfect on its own. If you rely solely on the Cyber Kill Chain, your analysis may lack flexibility. Attackers do not always follow a linear path — they skip stages, repeat steps, or pivot unexpectedly.
MITRE ATT&CK offers an extensive and highly detailed knowledge base, but it can be overwhelming. Without structure, it is easy to get lost in the details.
The Diamond Model, when used alone, can feel too conceptual or academic. Analysts may miss concrete tactical guidance during active investigations.

Using any of these frameworks in isolation can lead to incomplete results. Important details may be overlooked. However, when combined, they provide a comprehensive and layered approach for CTI professionals. Together, they help detect threats in time, measure the scope and context of an attack, understand adversary behavior, and present a clear overview to stakeholders. Properly applied, they offer a strong competitive advantage.

For example, an analyst may receive an alert at 2 PM from an EDR solution indicating malware activity communicating with a suspicious IP address.

- The Cyber Kill Chain can help determine how far the attack has progressed.
- MITRE ATT&CK can identify which tactics and techniques are being used.
- The Diamond Model can map the relationships between the adversary, infrastructure, capability, and victim.

Together, they transform a simple alert into structured, actionable intelligence.



#### Final Takeaway
There is no real competition between these frameworks. Each one solves a different problem: one tells the story of the attack, another provides behavioral and technical depth, and the third reveals relationships and context.

Frameworks do not stop ransomware or attacks — people and processes do. However, these models are essential pillars of a mature security program. Creating an environment where processes, people, and frameworks work together provides a decisive advantage.
